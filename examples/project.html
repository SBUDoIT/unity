Space Scheduling System

The Vice Provost for Undergraduate Colleges and Senior Vice President for Finance and Administration seek improved space and course scheduling capabilities for Stony Brook University. This is with the view to maximize utilization of available space in the university.

Background

This project will assess current processes and systems, analyze current state, design desired state, implement process and system improvements, upgrades and associated analytical reports and tools to establish robust capabilities for scheduling courses and events in Stony Brook University classrooms and other meeting spaces.

The objective of this project is to implement 25 Live by June 1, 2014 and to roll it out to faculty, and staff. An effective implementation will facilitate university educational goals and encourage scheduling efficiencies on campus.

Benefits

The objective of this project is to implement 25 Live by October 1, 2014 and to roll it out to faculty, and staff. An effective implementation will facilitate university educational goals and encourage scheduling efficiencies on campus.

Enable Stony Brook University to optimize use of classroom, meeting, and other spaces through consolidation of space usage onto one platform (25Live)

Simplify the room request/reservation and course scheduling process

Provide robust analytics to support decision-making on space needs and assignments, establishing a benchmark for efficient space usage (X25)

Provide a platform for managing security and energy usage based on information about when a space is in use.
